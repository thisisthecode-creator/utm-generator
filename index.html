Description: Identifies the marketing medium or channel type used to deliver the link.
Use Cases: Use to categorize the marketing channel. Common values: "email" (email campaigns), "cpc" (cost-per-click ads like Google Ads), "social" (social media posts), "organic" (organic search), "referral" (referral traffic), "display" (banner ads), "affiliate" (affiliate marketing), "sms" (text messages), "push" (push notifications)

Description: A unique identifier for a specific ad campaign, often used by advertising platforms.

Examples: "12345" (Google Ads campaign ID), "fb_67890" (Facebook campaign ID), "ad_set_abc123"
Description: Identifies the paid search keywords that triggered your ad, or other terms for tracking.

Examples: "running shoes", "best laptop 2024", "marketing software", "discount code". This helps you identify which keywords are most effective for your campaigns.
Description: Identifies what specific link or creative element was clicked to differentiate similar content.

This helps you optimize your marketing mix and understand which tactics drive the best results.

Description: Adds a template variable for email personalization in email marketing platforms.
Use Cases: When enabled, this adds &email={{email}} to your URL. Email marketing platforms (like Mailchimp, SendGrid, Klaviyo) will automatically replace {{email}} with each recipient's email address. This is useful for tracking individual email opens and clicks, personalizing landing pages, or pre-filling forms with the recipient's email.
Description: Adds a template variable for first name personalization in email marketing platforms.
Use Cases: When enabled, this adds &firstName={{firstName}} to your URL. Email marketing platforms will automatically replace {{firstName}} with each recipient's first name. This is useful for personalizing landing pages, greeting users by name, or tracking which contacts are engaging with your campaigns.
